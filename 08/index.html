8: CNC Milling, Molding, and Casting

This week was super fun to do, but very tedious. We had to design something and make it using CNC, and then create something by molding and casting. I decided to make a heart design in wax with the SRM-20 Mill.

Designing it fusion wasn't hard since I'd gotten the hang of it. I just had to take it to a thumbdrive, bring it the computer connected to the mill, and callibrate it. I played with the settings, and with Suvin's help I was able to get a pretty good cut. The edges were touching the rest of the wax, so it wouldn't have worked great to cast with, but I had a different plan for casting.

My idea for casting was to make a cast of my hand that could act as a jewelry holder! I got skin-safe molding material from Nathan and I got to work.

I read the instructions and watched a tutorial online, and I was confident that I could do it right the first time (spoiler alert- I didn't). I first measured equal parts water to powder.

Everything was going swimmingly... the mixture looked smooth and perfect... and then... I realized I didn't have enough. So, being the genius I am, I decided to measure more and pour it over. It didn't work and instantly made the mixture lumpy and gross and sadly I had to say goodbye.

For the second attempt, I made sure to measure plenty and cast it per instructions with warm water. It went well, it was still a bit lower than I would've liked and then I put my hand in.

It was a little painful because the position wasn't super comfortable but I managed. I waited then wiggled my finger out. I think my nail scratched it on the way out, but no way to tell until the casting is finished.

I decided to cast with plaster and to be honest, I messed it up majorly. I didn't read the instructions as well as I should've, and I got the wrong ratio and swapped the water and powder and it was just a mess.

The second time went well hopefully well though, but I first need to explain my idea. Since the base wasn't long enough, I decided to put a cup in to make it longer and sturdy. I didn't think to seal it though because I thought it would magically stay in place. It didn't and it leaked a bit, but I think it will be fine.

Finally, the results. I think it turned out well despite a big airbubble in the palm. The nails were a little rugged, but it was really detailed overall!!
